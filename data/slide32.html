    Margin collapse problem
    • Margin collapse happens when upper and lower margins of two butt-to-butt elements collapse into one (the greater one).
    • Most often, this is the desired behavior. Margin collapse will take place in 3 most frequent cases:
    • Neighboring elements:
      When two neighboring elements have vertical spacings - they will collapse to the value of the greater margin. A workaround-1: clear: left; float: left; /*('right' is also possible)*/ display: inline-block; jsFiddle
    • Parent element and the first/last child element:
      Usually, upper margin of the parent and child elements will collapse to the largest margin of the two. The same way the lower margins collapse for the last child element. A workaround-2: jsFiddle
      overflow: hidden; /*or any other, except for the 'visible'*/
      padding: 1px /*or any other value greater than 0*/
      border: 1px solid transparent /*или любой другой border*/
      float: left /*right will also work*/
